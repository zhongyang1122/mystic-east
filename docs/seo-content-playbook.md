# SEO Content Playbook

Mystic East should not publish low-effort AI filler. Every article must be useful to a real reader who is trying to understand BaZi in modern English.

## Google-Aligned Rules

- Write for people first, not only for rankings.
- Do not publish many pages that repeat the same generic explanation.
- Add original value: examples, chart interpretation, comparison tables, caveats, and a clear next step.
- Keep claims cautious. BaZi is framed as symbolic self-discovery, not medical, legal, financial, or mental health advice.
- Avoid fake expertise, fake testimonials, fake traffic, and fake case studies.
- Cite or link to internal product pages where useful, especially `calculator.html`.

## Required Article Structure

Each new article must include:

1. A specific search intent, stated in the opening paragraph.
2. One concrete example using BaZi terms, such as Day Master, birth hour, Five Elements, Ten Archetypes, or Four Pillars.
3. At least one table, checklist, or structured mini-guide that is useful even if the reader never buys anything.
4. A short "How to use this" section that turns the idea into reflection prompts.
5. One clear CTA to the free calculator.
6. A short disclaimer that this is symbolic self-reflection, not fixed fate.

## Do Not Publish

- Thin "What is X?" articles that only repeat definitions.
- Articles that are just keyword variations of existing pages.
- Articles that promise accuracy, destiny, guaranteed outcomes, wealth, marriage, health, or investment results.
- Articles with more SEO boilerplate than actual explanation.
- Articles without a specific reason to exist on this site.

## Topic Quality Test

Before creating an article, answer:

- What exact question is the reader trying to answer?
- What can Mystic East add that a generic article cannot?
- What will the reader be able to do after reading?
- Which existing page should this article link to?

If those answers are weak, pick a sharper topic.
